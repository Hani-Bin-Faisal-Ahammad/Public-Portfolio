Rank 1 | Shadow Slave

Guiltythree

4.57 / 5
★★★★☆
Source: Goodreads

Summary:

In a broken world of endless darkness, Sunny, a young man haunted by a tragic past, enters the Nightmare to earn a chance at a better life. He awakens in the world of the Spell, where he must fight horrifying creatures and collect strange abilities to survive. But the line between reality and the Nightmare begins to blur, and he must face his deepest fears to protect those he cares about.

My Thoughts:

Shadow Slave is a masterclass in world-building and character development. The narrative is dark and intense, but the protagonist's growth is incredibly rewarding to follow. The unique magic system and the unforgiving world make every chapter a suspenseful ride.

Rank 2 | Lord of Mysteries

Cuttlefish That Loves Diving

4.57 / 5
★★★★☆
Source: Goodreads

Summary:

Zhou Mingrui is reincarnated into a Victorian-era world filled with steampunk technology, ancient prophecies, and mystical abilities. He becomes Klein Moretti, a member of the Tarot Club, and embarks on a journey to uncover the secrets of this world. He must navigate a treacherous world of cults, conspiracies, and cosmic horror while trying to find a way back home.

My Thoughts:

Lord of Mysteries is one of the best novels I've ever read. The intricate plot, detailed world-building, and complex power system are unparalleled. The author masterfully weaves together detective fiction, horror, and fantasy into a truly epic story that keeps you hooked from beginning to end.

Rank 3 | Reverend Insanity

Gu Zhen Ren

4.55 / 5
★★★★☆
Source: Goodreads

Summary:

Fang Yuan, a ruthless and pragmatic cultivator, is reborn after 500 years of struggle and failure. With his previous life's memories and knowledge, he embarks on a new path to regain his former power and seek ultimate freedom. He must deal with conspiracies, rival cultivators, and a complex world where "gu" (a type of magical insect) determines one's strength and fate.

My Thoughts:

Reverend Insanity is a novel that challenges the conventions of its genre. The protagonist is not a hero, but a villain, and his amoral actions and logical approach to the world are fascinating to read. It's a dark and thought-provoking story that explores themes of morality and ambition in a brutal, but well-crafted, fantasy world.
